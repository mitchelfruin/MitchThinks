Summary

In the first section of The Origin of Wealth Beinhocker outlines 5 key differences between traditional (neoclassical) economics and complexity economics:

  1. Dynamics
  2. Agents
  3. Networks
  4. Emergence
  5. Evolution

I’ll try to illustrate each in turn (the emphasised summaries heading each section are taken from Beinhocker without adaptation).

1st difference: Dynamics

Traditional:

  • Closed, static, linear systems in equilibrium

Complexity:

  • Open, dynamic, non-linear systems far from equilibrium

2nd difference: Agents

Traditional:

  • Modeled collectively (homogenous)
  • Use complex deductive calculations to make decisions
  • Have complete information
  • Random errors (no biases)
  • No learning or adaptation (already optimal)

Complexity:

  • Modeled individually (heterogenous)
  • Use inductive heuristics to make decisions
  • Have incomplete information
  • Some biases
  • Learn and adapt over time

3rd difference: Networks

Traditional:

  • Agents only interact indirectly through market mechanisms

Complexity:

  • Explicitly model interactions between individual agents
  • Networks of relationships change over time

4th difference: Emergence

Traditional:

  • Separate disciplines for micro- and macroeconomics

Complexity:

  • No hard distinction between micro- and macro-
  • Macro patterns are emergent result of mirco-level behaviours and interactions

5th difference: Evolution

Traditional:

  • No mechanism for endogenously creating novelty, or growth in order/complexity

Complexity:

  • Process of differentiation, selection, and amplification generates novelty and is responsible for economy’s growth in order and complexity
