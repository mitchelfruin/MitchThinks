Estimating Pi 2000 years too late.

May 8, 2020 One minute read

I was given Strogatz’s Inifinite Powers as a christmas present (thanks Grandma). It’s a good book and I’d recommend it to anyone who ever sat in a maths class and genuinely asked the teacher, “err, but, like, why?” Most of the time, in my experience anyway, questions like this one don’t get a good answer. The reponse tended to be one of: a ‘why must you always interrupt me’ look, an exasperated “because it is”, or, worst of all, something along the lines of “because that’s the answer on the mark scheme and you need to know this for the exam”.

One of my favourite sections of the book invovled Strogatz recounting how Archimedes estimated the value of \(\pi\). The reason being that there’s a popular data science interview question which also requires the applicant to estimate \(\pi\). So, I thought it would be fun to compare Archimedes’ estimate to what I’ll be (shamelessly and repeatedly) calling Rchimedes’ estimate.
